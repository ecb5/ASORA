Observe that this is not a trivial generalization. Although the exceptional points are constrained to be finite in number, this number could still be extraordinarily large. That is, even if the series given above differed from zero on \(10^{10^{100000}}\) distinct points in the interval \((0, 10^{-10^{100000}})\) the coefficients still vanish. This remains true even if at each of these \(10^{10^{100000}}\) points the series converges to \(10^{10^{100000}}\text{.}\) This is truly remarkable when you think of it this way.
