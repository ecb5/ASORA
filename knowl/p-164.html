Leibniz started with subtraction. That is, if \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are very close together then their difference, \(\Delta x=x_2-x_1\text{,}\) is very small. He expanded this idea to say that if \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) are infinitely close together (but still distinct) then their difference, \(\dx{ x}\text{,}\) is infinitesimally small (but not zero).
