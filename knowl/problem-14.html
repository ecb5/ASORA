Problem 4.1.5.

(a)
Use Leibniz’s product rule \(\dx{ \left(xv\right)}=x\dx{ v}+v\dx{ x}\) to show that if \(n\) is a positive integer then \(\dx{ \left(x^n\right)}=nx^{n-1}\dx{ x}\)
(b)
Use Leibniz’s product rule to derive the quotient rule
\begin{equation*} \dx{ \left(\frac{v}{y}\right)}=\frac{y\,\dx{ v}-v\,\dx{ y}}{yy}\text{.} \end{equation*}
(c)
Use the quotient rule to show that if \(n\)is a positive integer, then
\begin{equation*} \dx{ \left(x^{-n}\right)}=-nx^{-n-1}\dx{ x}. \end{equation*}
