Theorem 9.2.1 will also handle quotients of continuous functions. There is however a small detail that needs to be addressed first. Obviously, when we consider the continuity of \(f/g\) at \(a\text{,}\)\(\)we need to assume that \(g(a)\neq 0\text{.}\) However, \(g\) may be zero at other values. How do we know that when we choose our sequence \(\left(x_n\right)\) converging to \(a\) that \(g(x_n)\) is not zero?
