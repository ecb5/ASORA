To do this, we examine an infinite sum by thinking of it as a sequence of finite partial sums. In our example, we would have the following sequence of partial sums.
\begin{equation*} \left(1,1+\frac{1}{2},1+\frac{1}{2}+\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2,1+ \frac{1}{2}+\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^3,\ldots,\sum_{j=0}^n\left(\frac{1}{2} \right)^j,\ldots\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
