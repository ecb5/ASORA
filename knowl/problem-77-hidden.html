Suppose \(\limit{n}{\infty}{a_n}=\infty\) and \(\limit{n}{\infty}{b_n}=-\infty\) and \(\alpha\in\RR\text{.}\) Prove or give a counterexample:

(a)

\(\limit{n}{\infty}{a_n+b_n}=\infty\)

(b)

\(\limit{n}{\infty}{a_nb_n}=-\infty\)

(c)

\(\limit{n}{\infty}{\alpha a_n}=\infty\)

(d)

\(\limit{n}{\infty}{\alpha b_n}=-\infty\)
