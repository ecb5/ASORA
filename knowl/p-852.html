From the graph, it appears that \(D(0) =1\) but we must be careful. \(D(0)\) doesn’t even exist! Somehow we must convey the idea that \(D(x)\) will approach \(1\) as \(x\) approaches \(0\text{,}\) even though the function is not defined at \(0\text{.}\) Cauchy’s idea was that the limit of \(D(x)\) would equal \(1\) because we can make \(D(x)\) differ from 1 by as little as we wish ([6], p. 158).
