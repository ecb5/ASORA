You might want to use Problem 7.4.8 of Chapter 7. Also there are two cases to consider: \(a\lt x\) and \(x\lt a\) (the case \(x=a\) is trivial). You will find that this is true in general. This is why we will often indicate that \(t\) is between \(a\) and \(x\) as in the theorem. In the case \(x\lt a\text{,}\) notice that
\begin{align*} \left|\int_{t=a}^xf^{(n+1)}(t)(x-t)^n\dx{ t}\right|\amp =\left|(-1)^{n+1}\int_{t=x}^af^{(n+1)}(t)(t-x)^n\dx{ t}\right|\\ \amp =\left|\int_{t=x}^af^{(n+1)}(t)(t-x)^n\dx{ t}\right|. \end{align*}
