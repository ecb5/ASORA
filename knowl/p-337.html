Although this systematic “machine” for obtaining power series for a function seems to have been known to a number of mathematicians in the early 1700’s, Brook Taylor was the first to publish this result in his Methodus Incrementorum (1715). The special case when \(a=0\) was included by Colin Maclaurin in his Treatise of Fluxions (1742). Thus when \(a=0\text{,}\) the series \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{f^{(n)}(0)}{n!}x^n\) is often called the Maclaurin Series for \(f\).
