The converse of Corollary 10.2.27 is not true as evidenced by the series \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{(-1)^n}{n+1}\text{.}\) As we noted in Chapter 4, this series converges to ln \(2\text{.}\) However, its absolute series is the Harmonic Series which diverges. Any such series which converges, but not absolutely, is said to converge conditionally. Recall also that in Chapter 4, we showed that we could rearrange the terms of the series \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{(-1)^n}{n+1}\) to make it converge to any number we wished. We noted further that all rearrangements of the series \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{(-1)^n}{\left(n+1\right)^2}\) converged to the same value.
