Problem 5.1.5. Cauchy’s Counterexample, Part 1.

(a)

Adopting the notation \(y=x^{-1}\) and \(f^{(n)}(x) =p_n(y)e^{-y^2}\text{,}\) find \(p_{n+1}(y)\) in terms of \(p_{n}(y)\text{.}\) [Note: Don’t forget that you are differentiating with respect to \(x\text{,}\) not \(y\text{.}\)]

(b)

Use induction on \(n\) to show that \(p_n(y)\) is a polynomial for all \(n\in\NN\text{.}\)
