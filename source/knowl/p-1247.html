The fact is that for a power series \(\sum a_nx^n\) with radius of convergence \(r\text{,}\) we know what happens for \(x\) with \(|x|\lt r\)and \(x\) with \(|x|>r\text{.}\) We never talked about what happens for \(x\) with \(|x|=r\text{.}\) That is because there is no systematic approach to this boundary problem. For example, consider the three series

\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty x^n,\sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}, \sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{x^{n+2}}{(n+1)(n+2)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
