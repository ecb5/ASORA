Continuing in this manner, we will produce two sequences, \(\left(x_n\right)\)and \(\left(y_n\right)\) satisfying the following conditions:

  1. \(\displaystyle x_1\leq x_2\leq x_3\leq\ldots\)

  2. \(\displaystyle y_1\geq y_2\geq y_3\geq\ldots\)

  3. \(\forall\) \(n\text{,}\) \(x_n\leq y_n\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\left(y_n-x_n\right)=\,\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{1}{2^{n-1}}\left(y_1-x_1\right)=0\)

  5. These sequences also satisfy the following property:

    \begin{equation*} \forall n,\,x_n^2\leq a\leq y_n^2 \end{equation*}
