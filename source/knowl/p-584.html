This is what happened with the words “open” and “closed.” These are opposites in English: “not open” is “closed,” “not closed” is “open,” and there is nothing which is both open and closed. But recall that an open interval on the real line, \((a,b)\text{,}\) is one that does not include either of its endpoints while a closed interval, \([a,b]\text{,}\) is one that includes both of them.
