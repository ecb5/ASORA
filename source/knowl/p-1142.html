Notice that for the Fourier series at the beginning of this chapter,

\begin{equation*} f(x)=\frac{4}{\pi}\left(\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2}x\right)-\frac{1}{3}\cos\left( 3\pi x\right)+\frac{1}{5}\cos\left(5\pi x\right)-\frac{1}{7}\cos\left(7\pi x\right)+\cdots\right) \end{equation*}

the convergence cannot be uniform on \((-\infty,\infty)\text{,}\) as the function \(f\) is not continuous. This never happens with a power series, since they converge to continuous functions whenever they converge. We will also see that uniform convergence is what allows us to integrate and differentiate a power series term by term.
