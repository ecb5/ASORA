Let \(n_0\) be sufficiently large that \(\frac{1}{n_0}\lt b-c\) and take \(\left(h_n\right)=\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)_{n=n_0}^\infty\text{.}\) Then \(f\left(c+\frac1n\right)-f(c) \leq 0\) and \(\frac1n>0\text{,}\) so that

\begin{equation*} \frac{f\left(c+h_n\right)-f(c)}{h_n}\leq 0, \ \ \forall n=n_0, n_0+1, \ldots \end{equation*}
