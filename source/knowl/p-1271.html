For example, if \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty(a_n\cos n\pi x\,+b_n\sin n\pi x)\) converges to \(f\) uniformly on the interval \((0,1)\text{,}\) then because of the uniform convergence, Fourier's term-by-term integration which we saw in PartÂ II is perfectly legitimate and the coefficients are, of necessity, the coefficients he computed. However we have seen that the convergence of a Fourier series need not be uniform. This does not mean that we cannot integrate term-by-term, but it does say that we can't be sure that term-by-term integration of a Fourier series will yield the integral of the associated function.
