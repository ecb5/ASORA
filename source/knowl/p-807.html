The function \(T(x)=\sin\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)\) is often called the topologist's sine curve. Whereas \(\sin x\) has roots at \(n\pi\text{,}\) \(n\in\ZZ\) and oscillates infinitely often as \(x\rightarrow\pm\infty\text{,}\) \(T\) has roots at \(\frac{1}{n\pi},\,n\in\ZZ,\,n\neq 0\text{,}\) and oscillates infinitely often as \(x\) approaches zero.
