Consider the sequence, \(\left(n\right)_{n=1}^\infty\text{.}\) This clearly diverges by getting larger and larger \(\ldots\) Ooops! Let's be careful. The sequence \(\left(1-\frac1n\right)_{n=1}^\infty\) gets larger and larger too, but it converges. What we meant to say was that the terms of the sequence \(\left(n\right)_{n=1}^\infty\) become arbitrarily large as \(n\) increases.
