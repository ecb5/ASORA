Theorem 9.4.8.

Rolle's Theorem

Suppose \(f^\prime\) exists for every \(x\in(a,b)\text{,}\) \(f\) is continuous on \([a,b]\text{,}\) and

\begin{equation*} f(a)=f(b) \text{.} \end{equation*}

Then there is a real number \(c\in(a,b)\) such that

\begin{equation*} f^\prime(c)=0 \text{.} \end{equation*}
