Proof

Let \(\eps>0\text{.}\) Let \(\delta=\eps\text{.}\) If \(|\,x-1|\lt \delta\text{,}\) then \(|\,x-1|\lt \eps\text{,}\) and so

\begin{equation*} \abs{\sqrt{x}-\sqrt{1}}=|\frac{\left(\sqrt{x}-1\right)\left(\sqrt{x}+1\right)}{ \sqrt{x}+1}|=\frac{|x-1|}{\sqrt{x}+1}\lt \abs{x-1}\lt \eps\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus by definition, \(f(x)=\sqrt{x}\) is continuous at \(1\text{.}\)
