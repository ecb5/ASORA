Let \(\left(x_n\right)\) be a sequence such that \(x_n\ne a\) and \(\limit{n}{\infty}{x_n}=a\text{.}\) Since \(\limit{x}{a}{f(x)} = L\) and \(\limit{x}{a}{g(x)} = M\) we see that \(\limit{n}{\infty}{f(x_n)} = L\) and \(\limit{n}{\infty}{g(x_n)} = M\text{.}\) By Theorem 7.2.4 of Chapter 7, we have \(\limit{n}{\infty}{f(x_n)+g(x_n)}=L+M\text{.}\) Since \(\left\{x_n\right\}\) was an arbitrary sequence with \(x_n\ne a\) and \(\limit{n}{\infty}{x_n} = a\) we have

\begin{equation*} \limit{x}{a}{f(x)+g(x)} = L+M\text{.} \end{equation*}
